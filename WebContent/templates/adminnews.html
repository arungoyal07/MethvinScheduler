Pricing with Sub-schedules.

Limitless detail, infinite possibilities.

When the client provides one item for a complex section of work,  for example, a Lump Sum Item calls for the construction of a bridge. If you change the item into a sub-schedule item by clicking on the icon at the top of the Pricing Module, any items pertaining to the sub-section item will roll up the clients item when printing out this report. Under the sub-schedule you are able to add as much detail as you require, to allow you to price the item accurately. The Client report will reflect only the Lump sum as dictated by the sub- schedule, in this case ?Bridge?.

Advanced pricing options.

Elementary or complex pricing. Or the best of both.

Simple Pricing

Insert your required gross rate directly into pricing tabs gross rate column. This can then be printed as a client's quote. This saves automatically and you have successfully created your first tender in Methvin.

Complex Worksheet Pricing

Under each item in the schedule there is a worksheet dynamically attached. Here you can drag in resources or worksheets from the accordion menu on the right side of the pricing module, decreasing your pricing time and improving the accuracy of your tender.

Pricing with variables.

Good software supports your workflow. Great software improves it.

The complex worksheet allows you to set variables for pricing, for example the distance from a quarry to the site or the depth of a trench. When you set up a new project, with a similar item to price,  all that is required is to adjust the variables to the new circumstances.

The function column facilitates the use of any mathematical function. Caution,  watch the brackets when using complicated equations.

Java Pricing

The worlds most advanced pricing system.

Unique to Methvin, you are able to utilise any function available in Java to automate your complex pricing.

Examples:

  • if ([your variable i.e. 2]>5) {yes} else {no} - Returns no

  • Math.round(2.5) - Returns 3

  • var x = Math.PI - Returns PI

  • var y = Math.sqrt(16) - Returns the square root of 16

Project Management

Monthly Claim

Monthly applications for payment are produced by entering either a cumulative progress quantity or a percentage complete against changed bill items. The progress quantity buildup can be recorded using measurement sheets. The available progress quantities are Claimed, Paid and Actual, and a number of derived quantities, such as Quantity to Completion, are also provided.

Communication distribution

Quickly distribute project plans, specs, and addenda, as well as construction schedules, meeting minutes, or any other documents that need to be shared with the project team.
